Introduction

Imagine a world where computer vision-based systems can analyze a video of an athlete, a surgeon, a patient, or a factory worker and instantly provide expert-level actionable feedback---correcting techniques, identifying inefficiencies, and helping people refine their skills in real time. Thanks to rapid progress in video understanding, this vision is becoming reality. AI-powered systems can now analyze complex human activities, assess performance, and generate intelligent feedback, unlocking new possibilities in sports, healthcare, manufacturing, education, rehabilitation, and beyond. Through Expert Keynotes and Invited Contributions, this ICCV 2025 workshop will explore the cutting edge of skilled activity understanding, assessment, and feedback generation, bridging research and real-world applications.


As AI systems become more capable of understanding human expertise, the implications are profound---empowering individuals with personalized coaching, democratized skill development, and scalable training solutions. We invite researchers, industry leaders, and practitioners to join us in shaping the future of AI-powered skill understanding. Whether working on foundational research, applied solutions, or real-world deployment, this workshop is an opportunity and forum to learn about and push the boundaries of how AI perceives, evaluates, and enhances human ability.

Call for Papers

We invite contributions related to, but not limited to, the following areas:

  • Algorithms & Applications for Skilled Human Activity and Performance Assessment

    • Human Skills/Performance/Action Quality Assessment/Evaluation in various domains such as Sports, Industry/Assembly line, Musical and Dance performance, Surgical procedures, Fitness, Robotics, Gait Assessment, Rehabilitation, Healthcare etc.
    • Action and Process Mistake Detection using video and video analytics
    • Missing Action Prediction and Alternative Action Sequence Prediction.
    • Brave new ideas and novel applications of Action Quality Assessment in: Video Generation, Human-Robot-Collaboration, Robot Skills Learning etc.

  • Feedback Report Generation for Skills/Performance Improvement & Guidance

    • Captioning and Narration for Natural Language Corrective Feedback Generation
    • Visual/AR/VR-based Corrective Feedback Generation

  • New Datasets & Benchmarks

    • Datasets and Benchmarks
    • Multimodal, Multiview datasets
    • Creation of Synthetic Fine-grained Action datasets
    • Fine-grained Video/Action generation

  • Metric learning for Skills/Action/Motion Quality Assessment

  • Fine-grained Action Recognition & Segmentation

  • Representation Learning & Learning Paradigms

    • Pose Estimation and Human-Centric Representation Learning, especially, suitable for challenging scenarios such as high-speed, non-regular actions, occlusions, etc.
    • Generalization/Transfer Learning/Domain Adaptation for SAUAFG
    • Semi/Self-Supervised Learning for SAUAFG
    • Continual/Incremental Skills and Action Quality Assessment
    • Incorporation of Knowledge Graphs
    • Learnable Task Graphs

  • Explainability & Interpretability in SAUAFG models

  • Ethics & Fairness in SAUAFG

    • Ethical Considerations, Fairness, and Bias in SAUAFG systems, and strategies to mitigate them.
    • Social Impact of SAUAFG, including its ability to democratize access to training and Performance Evaluations, particularly in underserved communities.
